Tax Distribution

Discover the journey of your tax rupees at CityInsight. Explore clear visualizations and insights into how your taxes shape education, healthcare, defense, and more. Empower yourself with knowledge and impact. Join us in fostering informed citizenship and positive change.

As depicted in the graph on the side, the majority of the revenue received every fiscal year comes from Borrowings & Other liabilities, which is the money the government borrows. Second to which is GST (the Goods & Service Tax). Following it are the Corporation and Income tax at 15%. The majority of the government's revenue comes from taxes paid either by consumers or corporations.

This graph depicts the expenditure done by the government on a larger scale, majority is used in Interest Payments, followed by distribution amongst the states with their share of tax duties. that is followed by Central government schemes, finance and defence sectors.

This square pie chart again shows the expenditure of money by the government in more depth, it also displays different schemes where funds go, that is the PM-KISAN scheme. Defence, transport, tax administration, home affairs are some major departs where funds go.

This bar graph shows the trend of major items of expenditure for a three year period. We can see a sharp rise in area such as Transport, which is due to the upcoming development of better public transport throughout the country. The is also a rise in areas of Education, Social Welfare and Health. There has been a decrease in areas such as Urban Development and Rural Development.
